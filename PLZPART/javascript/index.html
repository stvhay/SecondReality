Second Reality — PLZ plasma (JavaScript port)

Source & notes Repository

This recreates the plasma part listed in SCRIPT immediately before Plasmacube. It keeps the original lookup-table formula, phase speeds, palette fades, 70 Hz stepping, VGA planar checkerboard interleave, and the 320×400 line-compare presentation.

Monitor sends that signal through a WebGL2 shader modelled on a 1992 NEC MultiSync-class shadow-mask monitor: CRT-gamma decode, vertical scan beam, staggered shadow-mask triads, horizontal phosphor spread, and a separable halation bloom. Geometry is left flat — no curvature, no warp. Signal shows the raw 320×400 framebuffer at square-pixel aspect, including the line-compare drop between palette transitions. Plasma is just the 320×280 chunky pixel buffer the assembly renderer fills into video memory, before the VGA mode places it on screen.
