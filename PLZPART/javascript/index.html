This recreates the plasma part listed in SCRIPT immediately before Plasmacube. It keeps the original lookup-table formula, phase speeds, palette fades, 70 Hz stepping, VGA planar checkerboard interleave, and the 320×400 line-compare presentation.

Monitor upscales the VGA signal to the display with a slight Gaussian blur sized in source-pixel units (sigma under one source pixel, FWHM just under one). Just enough smoothing to take the staircase off nearest-neighbour scaling without smearing neighbouring source pixels into each other. No scanlines, no mask, no halation. Signal shows the raw 320×400 framebuffer drawn into the same 4:3 box a real VGA monitor would have used, including the line-compare drop between palette transitions. Plasma is the 320×280 chunky pixel buffer the assembly renderer fills into video memory, before the VGA mode places it on screen.
